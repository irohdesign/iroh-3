I start my designs on paper. Wireframes then move to Sketch, where I build basic prototypes and flesh them out to best fit the content. Building the product around the content ensures that the main goal is being accomplished.

How did you get started in design?

I took AP Photography in High School and fell in love with the artistic computer process. I would slip on a pair of headphones and work in Adobe Photoshop and Illustrator for hours. I'd always been a creative kid, coming up with new uses for old projects, but computers were a new frontier.

My life follows my passion for information. That passion carried me through my Bachelors degree in History, into my position as a Technical Writer for a State Department Contractor, and ultimately, here. I moved into graphic design during my internships, starting with presentations and moving upward into coding and information design. It was there that I found my true passion: Web Design and Development.

Web Design and Development are perfect for me because they're constantly evolving. Just like History, there’s no end in sight. New technologies and languages emerge every day, and that’s all I want to do: keep learning.

Passion Projects?

I’m currently studying Adobe After Effects and I want to create a series of fun and weird educational videos. I've been animating in CSS and Javascript, but I think that After Effects is my gateway to a whole new level of possibilities.

I’ve been working with scalable vector graphics (SVG) for a few months, and I’m excited to experiment with After Effects animations exported into SVG. I think that animation, whether in small UI or large illustrative applications, is the future of User Experience and that's where I want to be.

Special Skills?

I do hand lettering, am very good at softball, and can juggle. I also play a few instruments and can't wait to get my hands on a Mandocello.

I think that my most valuable skill is being okay with being bad at things. It might sound odd, but I get so excited when I start something that I’m terrible at. Instead of seeing it as an embarrassment or a failure, I see it as an opportunity to learn more cool stuff. I’m sad when I hit a ceiling or when there’s nothing more to learn.

Web Design and Development are both so exciting to me because there is no end. There’s always someone better than you that you can learn from, and everyone’s methods are unique.

What're You Looking Forward To?

I’m looking forward to being around ambitious and passionate people. I’m just starting out in my career, and I have no idea where it’s going, and that’s fantastic. I can’t wait to find people who I can learn from and grow with.

I love creating. Whether it’s music, sports, design or code, my main goal in life is to make things. It doesn’t matter if the project makes money or gets attention, I just like challenging myself and picking up new information along the way.
